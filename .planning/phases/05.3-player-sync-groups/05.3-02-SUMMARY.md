## Decisions Made
- `last_data_time` initialized to `None` (not `Some(Instant::now())`): a relay_active=true with no data ever sent does not trigger the health-check, avoiding spurious clears at startup
- Health-check is a **secondary** defense only — `RelayGuard::drop` at line 601 is the primary mechanism; the timeout covers the edge case where tokio task cancellation skips drop
- `Option<Instant>` inside `Mutex` (not `AtomicU64` epoch millis): simpler, avoids epoch-math, `std::sync::Mutex` is already in scope and appropriate for a low-frequency read/write
