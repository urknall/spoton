<task type="auto">
  <name>Task 2: Add diagnostic timing logs to Connect event handler</name>
  <files>Plugins/SpotOn/Connect.pm</files>
  <action>
  In Connect.pm, add diagnostic timing logs to the _connectEvent handler. These logs
  are guarded by the diagnosticMode pref and use a [DIAG] prefix for easy grep filtering.

  1. At the top of _connectEvent (after the existing "Got spottyconnect event" log on
     line 488), add a diagnostic block:

     my $diagMode = $prefs->get('diagnosticMode');
     my $diagTs = $diagMode ? sprintf('%.3f', Time::HiRes::time()) : '';

     Then after each command handler section (start, change, stop, resume, volume, seek),
     add diagnostic logging. Place the diag log INSIDE each handler block, right before
     the return statement:

     For 'start' (inside the if block, before final return):
       $log->warn("[DIAG] [$diagTs] start: player=" . $client->id
         . " track=" . ($request->getParam('_p2') || 'none')
         . " elapsed=" . sprintf('%.3f', Time::HiRes::time() - $diagTs)
       ) if $diagMode;

     For 'change' (before return):
       $log->warn("[DIAG] [$diagTs] change: player=" . $client->id
         . " prev=" . ($request->getParam('_p3') || 'none')
         . " new=" . ($request->getParam('_p2') || 'none')
         . " elapsed=" . sprintf('%.3f', Time::HiRes::time() - $diagTs)
       ) if $diagMode;

     For 'stop' (before each return in the stop block — both the grace-period return
     and the normal pause return):
       $log->warn("[DIAG] [$diagTs] stop: player=" . $client->id
         . " isPlaying=" . ($client->isPlaying ? 1 : 0)
         . " gracePeriod=" . ($graceSkipped ? 1 : 0)
         . " elapsed=" . sprintf('%.3f', Time::HiRes::time() - $diagTs)
       ) if $diagMode;
     (Set a local $graceSkipped flag in the grace period check block.)

     For 'resume' (before each return in the resume block):
       $log->warn("[DIAG] [$diagTs] resume: player=" . $client->id
         . " track=" . ($request->getParam('_p2') || 'none')
         . " position=" . ($request->getParam('_p3') || 'none')
         . " actuallyInConnect=" . ($actuallyInConnect ? 1 : 0)
         . " elapsed=" . sprintf('%.3f', Time::HiRes::time() - $diagTs)
       ) if $diagMode;

  Important: Use $log->warn (not $log->info or $log->debug) so diagnostic lines appear
  at the default WARN log level without requiring users to change their log level.
  The [DIAG] prefix distinguishes these from real warnings.

  The $diagTs variable is captured once at handler entry. The "elapsed" field shows
  how long the handler spent processing, which helps identify where the 20s delay occurs.
  </action>

  Connect.pm compiles cleanly. At least 4 [DIAG] log lines exist (start, change, stop, resume).
